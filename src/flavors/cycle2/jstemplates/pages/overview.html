{{#_}}Welcome to Ideas in Action!{{/_}}

{{#_}}Ideas in Action is Boston’s Participatory Budgeting (PB) initiative, where residents can decide how to spend a part of the City’s budget. By engaging in Ideas in Action, Bostonians of all ages and backgrounds can:

  1. Share project ideas that help advance social and racial equity;
  2. Decide on the top projects that will appear on the PB Ballot for voting; and,
  3. Vote on how to spend $2 million of the City’s budget to benefit our communities.
{{/_}}

{{#_}}As part of this process, residents inform the City about their priorities to help decide future budget investments.{{/_}}

{{#_}}When can I start sharing my ideas{{/_}}

{{#_}}Cycle Two of Ideas in Action starts this summer! Between July 1st and July 31, you can share project ideas through this website, over the phone by calling (617) 635-3059, or in-person at Boston Public Library Branches and through community events across the City.{{/_}}

{{#_}}Tell us: How would you spend $2 million to benefit our communities?{{/_}}

{{#_}}What happens after I share my ideas?{{/_}}

{{#_}}From August through September 2025, the City will review all the submitted project ideas. Then, in the fall, the Office of Participatory Budgeting will host public workshops for residents to review, prioritize, and develop these ideas into project proposals for the PB ballot. Voting will take place in January of 2026!{{/_}}

{{#_}}How do I stay connected?{{/_}}

{{#_}}Sign up for our newsletter! For more information about Participatory Budgeting, please visit Boston.gov/participate or contact the Office of Participatory Budgeting at pb@boston.gov.{{/_}}

{{#_}}Idea Submission Criteria{{/_}}

{{#_}}Who can submit project ideas?{{/_}}

{{#_}}All City of Boston residents, regardless of age, can submit ideas.{{/_}}

{{#_}}What type of ideas can I propose?{{/_}}

  • {{#_}}Project ideas that are limited to a one-time expense, and do not create new permanent positions;{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Ideas about programs, services, or physical infrastructure improvements;{{/_}}
    • {{#_}}Infrastructure projects on private, state, or federal property are not eligible, such as projects concerning the Department of Conservation & Recreation (DCR) or the Massachusetts Bay Transit Authority (MBTA).{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Project ideas that are feasible to implement, and are consistent with existing City policies, laws, regulations, and the Participatory Budgeting Ordinance;{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Lastly, projects must be implemented or managed by the appropriate City Department or agency receiving direct budget appropriations.{{/_}}

{{#_}}Project ideas containing inappropriate, offensive, or derogatory language will be removed and not considered from the PB process.{{/_}}

{{#_}}Examples of eligible project ideas:{{/_}}

  • {{#_}}Programming to strengthen mental health among Boston youth;{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Expand the City’s Wicked Free WiFi network to reach more low-income areas;{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Digital literacy classes for senior citizens to learn new skills and promote social connections;{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Enhance public spaces through infrastructure projects (public art, street trees, benches, etc.){{/_}}

{{#_}}Examples of ineligible project ideas:{{/_}}

  • {{#_}}Paying for the salary of a public employee to provide services;{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Repairing a privately owned building;{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Helping an organization renovate a space that it rents from a private landlord;{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Helping a religious institution repair its sanctuary;{{/_}}
  • {{#_}}Increase the frequency of MBTA buses or trains.{{/_}}
